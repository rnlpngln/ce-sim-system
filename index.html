What is Civil Engineering?

Civil engineering is the discipline responsible for designing, constructing, and maintaining infrastructure such as buildings, bridges, roads, dams, and water systems. It combines physics, mathematics, and environmental science to ensure structures are safe, efficient, and sustainable.

Major Fields of Civil Engineering

  • Structural Engineering: Buildings, bridges, towers
  • Geotechnical Engineering: Soil mechanics, foundations
  • Transportation Engineering: Roads, traffic systems
  • Hydraulic Engineering: Water flow, dams, drainage
  • Environmental Engineering: Waste, pollution control
  • Construction Engineering: Project management
  • Coastal Engineering: Shore protection
  • Earthquake Engineering: Seismic design
  • Urban Engineering: City planning systems
