Core Concepts

  • Stress (σ): Force per unit area
  • Strain (ε): Deformation ratio
  • Elasticity: Ability to return to original shape
  • Plasticity: Permanent deformation
  • Young’s Modulus: Stiffness indicator

Types of Loads

  • Dead Load – constant structural weight
  • Live Load – temporary loads
  • Impact Load – sudden forces
  • Dynamic Load – moving forces
  • Thermal Load – expansion/contraction
  • Environmental Load – wind, flood

Equilibrium

  • Σ Forces = 0
  • Σ Moments = 0
  • Structure remains stable

Structural Analysis

  • Load distribution analysis
  • Reaction forces calculation
  • Internal forces (shear, bending)

Failure Theories

  • Maximum Stress Theory
  • Maximum Strain Theory
  • Von Mises Theory
  • Fatigue Failure Theory

Factor of Safety

  • FoS Formula: Strength / Applied Stress
  • Interpretation: Higher FoS = safer design
  • Trade-off: Higher FoS = increased cost and material usage

Engineering Importance

Understanding fundamentals ensures that every design is safe, efficient, and reliable under all conditions.
